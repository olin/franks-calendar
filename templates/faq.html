Frequently Asked Questions

Filtering Events

How are events categorized?

When an event is published via the online form, the organizer will manually assign a category to the event before submitting it for approval.

What category does this event belong to?

Here’s an overview of what some categories mean:

Academic Affairs

Any event that is related to academics at Olin

Sub-filters are Academic Calendar and Academic Support

Student Affairs

Any event that is related to student affairs at Olin

Sub-filters are Residential, Health & Wellness, PGP, HR, Intl’ & Study Away, and Diversity & Inclusion

Admission & Financial Aid

Any event related to or hosted by Admission and Financial Aid, e.g. Candidate’s Weekend or Financial Aid hours (as long as these hours are open to the whole community)

The Library

Any event related to or hosted by the Library, e.g. a Book Talk with an author

The Shop

Any event related to or hosted by the Shop, e.g. a “Chips and Guac” session with NINJAs

Clubs & Organizations

Any event hosted by student clubs on campus that are open to the whole community, e.g. a PInT speaker event or a Formula info session for interested students

Other Events

Any Oliner-hosted event not related to any of the above categories but still open to the whole Olin community, e.g. a COVID update or an individually hosted workshop
What if the event is a collaboration between two of the filter groups?

In that case, please pick a single category that best suits the intention of the event!

For example, if the event is titled “Equity in the Workspace” and is co-hosted by Diversity & Inclusion and PGP, “Diversity & Inclusion” might be the more fitting category for the event. However, know that it can always be indicated in the “What?” section of the event that it is a collaboration between two (or more) departments.

Are there any other tags or filters other than the ones on the sidebar right now?

Not yet! If this feature sounds useful, let the moderators know in the feedback form!

Adding/Exporting Events

How are events added to Frank's Calendar?

Fill out the online form to submit an event for display on Frank's Calendar, and wait for an email from the moderators! Moderators will reply within 24 hours either with a notification of approval, a request for changes to be made, or a cancellation notice if the event doesn't adhere to Frank's Calendar guidelines.

How can an event be exported to a personal calendar?
  1. Click into an event in the main calendar page -- it will bring up a page with more event details, pictured above
  2. At the bottom, there will be a text field for an email address. Enter the email address of the preferred calendar platform (i.e. @gmail.com for a Google calendar or @olin.edu for an Outlook calendar.
  3. Hit “Send me an iCal”
  4. Wait for an email with an event invitation and click "Yes" in the RSVP field.
What happens if an exported event is edited or deleted on Frank’s Calendar?

If an event detail or timing has changed, Frank’s Calendar will send an email containing an updated event invitation.

Can events be submitted to Frank's Calendar from Google/Outlook calendar directly?

Not yet! If this feature sounds useful, let the moderators know in the feedback form!

About the Project

How was Frank's Calendar founded?

Frank’s Calendar was founded through the last three sprints of Products & Markets in Spring 2020. The original team consisted of Ruby Eisenbud '23, Jules Brettle '23, Jack Greenberg '23, and Karen Hinh '23, who took the project from initial ideation and market research to full product development.

Over the summer, the project expanded to include Kyle Combes '20 as a mentor, and added Kelly Yen '23 as a back-end developer. Raquel Dunoff '21 joined near the end to help with launch, and will be staying on with the new Frank's Calendar team.

The MVP was built and launched in time for Fall 2020.

Why was this calendar built?

Oliners receive a lot of emails, especially when it comes to events being shared with the whole community. Whether the email is packed full of event details or a simple, “HAPPENING NOW!” notification, it can be hard to keep track of all of this information.

Past attempts at building a community calendar for Olin have failed because they weren’t ubiquitous enough. The process to make icals or add events was also too much work on those platforms.

That’s why Frank’s Calendar was built: it takes all of the community-wide events on campus and puts them in one place. Its’ main design focus is ease-of-use for both event browsers and hosters.

What's its main purpose?

The platform easily allows any member of the Olin Community to:

  1. See what events are coming up
  2. Share events they are hosting
  3. Export events they are interested in to their calendar.

The calendar was designed for people looking for events to attend in the upcoming day or week. It is also for event organizers to see what events have already been scheduled, and find a conflict-free time for their own event.
